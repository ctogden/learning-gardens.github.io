What is the Nursery Grants Program?

The Learning Gardens Nursery Grants Program exists to financially support small projects that enact, advance, and/or celebrate decentralized and peer-led learning.

Learning Gardens distributes awards in the $50-$300 range. Amounts requested must correspond directly to purchases or hours recorded towards the project.

We're doing this for the first time and ironing out the kinks. For Nursery Grants vol. 1, the application period is April 1-30, 2017. Recipients will be determined by April 15th, and funds distributed immediately thereafter.

Who is eligible for a Nursery Grant?

Anyone. You do not need to be a part of Learning Gardens already (though we will require you to join our Slack!).

You do not need to have a background in this kind of stuff. We will ask for any previous work that can attest to your ability to follow-through on a project, though.

You can apply for a Nursery grant as an individual, as a team, or as an organization.

People of color, women, LGBTQ persons, people of low socioeconomic status, and those without a history of formal institutional support will receive first priority for Nursery Grants.

What We're Looking For

We are looking for concrete, implementable projects with a finite timeline (i.e. you should be able to say "the project will be done at X date/time").

We are looking for projects that probe unexplored methodologies of teaching, sharing, and learning together.

We are looking for projects that address the nature of the world in 2017.

We are looking for projects that enhance existing projects, for projects that could lead to more, and for projects that start new things from scratch alike.

What's Necessary to Apply

  1. Pitch of project and its goals. (is it part of something larger? have you already started? what would successful implementation look like?)
  2. Statement on why a Nursery Grant is appropriate. (how does your project resonate with Learning Gardens?)
  3. Plan for implementation, with approximate timeline. (what steps will you take to implement, and when?)
  4. Documentation Plan for project. (how will you record and share your progress?)
  5. Budget spreadsheet with amount requested.
  6. Usual Information
